What is N26 Research?

N26 Research is our internal research hub. Our aim is to accelerate the pace of innovation within our organization by enabling knowledge sharing and efficient collaboration through effortless discovery of insights. All research displayed here resides on our Github repository Boston (only available for those with access to the N26 Github).

In order to contribute your work to Boston you need:

  1. Access to N26 Github and the Boston repository
  2. Python notebook or R file
  3. Either an external presentation link such as a Google Slides URL or the HTML output of your notebooks (both is fine too!)

For guidelines on how to prepare your research documents for upload, such as how to fill in the metadata in your notebook header, as well as how to actually upload the research such that it can be represented here, please consult the README.md file in the Boston repository.

How can I contribute?

To enable Boston to parse your research for the relevant metadata correctly, please follow the guidelines below. The more descriptive your metadata is, the better discoverable is your research on our Research Hub, so don‚Äôt sleep on this one!

  1. Fill in this sheet with your research metadata (1 line per research article)
    1. region: use the drop-down validation to select a region
    2. date: follow the format YYYY-MM-DD
    3. author: the research author‚Äôs name, probably yours
    4. link: URL to relevant materials of your research (e.g., Google Slides URL)
    5. tags: comma separated, please try to include as many searchable and relevant tags as possible
    6. summary: give an overview of what your research is about and what the main findings are. Other questions you may want to answer could include: what was the context of the research? How were the findings made actionable? What were next step recommendations? What were limitations?
    7. segment: use the drop-down validation to select a segment
  2. Get in touch with a Data Analyst (either message your go-to Data Analyst, or a random pick from here, or file a ticket) to upload your research and add it to the Research Hub. They will convert your input into a CSV file and add it to Boston, our research repository on Github.
